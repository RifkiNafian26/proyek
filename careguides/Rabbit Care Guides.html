Rabbit Care
Guides

Fresh Hay & Veggies Meals

Make sure your rabbit always has unlimited fresh hay to support digestion and dental health, provide clean water daily, and offer a balanced mix of safe vegetables and high-quality rabbit pellets while avoiding harmful foods like chocolate, iceberg lettuce, and anything sugary or processed.

Hopping, Exploring & Enrichment Fun

Help your rabbit stay happy and active by giving them plenty of space to hop, run, and explore, offering toys such as tunnels, chew sticks, cardboard boxes, and puzzle feeders to stimulate their curiosity and natural behavior while giving them supervised time outside their enclosure every day.

Soft Fur & Safe Nails

Keep your rabbit comfortable by gently grooming their fur (especially if they’re long-haired), trimming their nails as needed, and maintaining a clean environment by spot-cleaning the enclosure daily, changing bedding regularly, and providing a litter box with rabbit-safe litter for hygiene and comfort.

Safe Home, Gentle Handling & Vet Care

Protect your rabbit’s health by scheduling regular vet visits, ensuring vaccinations and parasite prevention, handling them carefully and calmly to avoid stress or injury, and providing a safe indoor space free from hazards like wires, toxic plants, or rough handling.
