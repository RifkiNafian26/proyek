Dog Care
Guides

Tasty Treats & Healthy Meals

Make sure to feed your dog high-quality food that matches its age, size, and breed, give meals on a regular schedule, always keep clean fresh water available, offer treats in moderation, and avoid harmful human foods such as chocolate, grapes, onions, and anything overly salty or spicy to protect their health.

Playtime, Walks, and Adventures

Help your dog stay happy and healthy by giving them daily exercise like walks, running, or playing fetch, and include fun activities such as puzzle toys, tug-of-war, or social time at the dog park to keep their body active and their mind engaged, which also strengthens the family bond.

Pampering, Brushing & Fresh Smells

Take good care of your dog's hygiene by brushing their coat regularly to remove loose hair, giving them baths every few weeks with dog-safe shampoo, trimming their nails before they become too long, brushing their teeth several times a week, and gently cleaning or checking their ears to prevent dirt, odor, or infections.

Happy, Safe & Vet-Approved Care

Keep your dog safe and well by scheduling routine vet visits for check-ups and vaccinations, staying on top of flea, tick, and worm prevention, using positive reinforcement during training so they learn good behavior, and providing a secure environment with a cozy resting area and no access to dangerous items.
